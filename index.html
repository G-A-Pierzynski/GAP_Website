Hi! I'm Gabriel, an incoming PhD student in Economic Geography at the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE), starting in September. My doctoral research, advised by Neil Lee and Vassilis Monastiriotis, investigates the economics of digital technology diffusion — why digital technologies cluster spatially, and the role of human capital, competition, and industrial policy in how they spread. I work primarily with econometric and data-science methods.

Alongside my PhD, I am a Research Associate in Economics and Data Science at LSE, where I lead the data work across several empirical economics projects. My broader interests sit at the intersection of computational social science, economic geography, and international relations theory. Before this, I was a Blue Book Trainee at the European Commission, working on economic analysis and the European Semester.

I obtained an MSc in European and International Public Policy (Political Economy) from the University of Copenhagen's partner LSE (2024) and a BSc in Philosophy and Politics from the University of Bristol (2023). When I'm not at the keyboard, you'll often find me at sea — I once built a satellite SMS weather-routing system for blue-water sailors. I work in English and Polish.
